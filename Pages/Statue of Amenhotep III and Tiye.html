Statue of Amenhotep III and Tiye

Story & Age

The "status" focuses on the physical condition and characteristics of the mummies themselves, as they were found outside of their original elaborate coffins.

Current Status

amenhotep is Identified as "The Elder Mummy" (or simply one of the unnamed mummies) found in the cache. It is now housed at the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC). Condition: The mummy is rather poorly preserved, especially the soft tissues on the skull, indicating significant post-mortem damage or reburial processes. tiye is Identified as "The Elder Lady" found in the cache. The identification was confirmed via DNA analysis in 2010. She is also housed at the NMEC. Condition: The mummy is relatively well-preserved despite signs of ancient damage and handling (such as being found nude and unwrapped). Her left arm is flexed in the royal pose across her chest.

Discovery & Location

The French archaeologist Victor Loret. Discovery Date: 1898 AD. Location: The mummies were not found in Amenhotep III's original tomb (WV22). Instead, they were discovered hidden in a side chamber within the Tomb of Amenhotep II (KV35) in the Valley of the Kings.
