Golden Mask of Tutankhamun

Story & Age

Tutankhamun's mask is a stunning masterpiece crafted from extremely valuable materials

Great Achievements

Primary Material Solid Gold (high purity). The mask weighs approximately 11 kilograms (about 25 pounds). Gems and Decorations The mask is inlaid and adorned with a large number of semi-precious stones and colored glass: - Hair, Eyebrows, and Eyeliner Blue Lapis Lazuli stone. - Eyes White Quartz and Obsidian. - Ceremonial Beard Made of Gold and inlaid with Blue Glass to appear braided. Symbols The mask is topped with two important symbols: the Vulture (Nekhbet) and the Cobra (Uraeus/Wadjet), representing the Pharaoh's protection and his rule over the Two Lands (Upper and Lower Egypt).

Discovery & Location

Chief Discoverer: The British archaeologist Howard Carter. Discovery Date of the Tomb: The entire tomb (Tomb KV62 in the Valley of the Kings) was discovered on November 4, 1922.
