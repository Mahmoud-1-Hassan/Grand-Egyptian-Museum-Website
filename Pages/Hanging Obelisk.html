Luxor Obelisk

Story & Age

The colossal Statue of Ramses II was carved from a single block of granite during the 13th century BCE, representing one of Egypt’s greatest pharaohs. Originally placed in Memphis, it symbolizes royal power and divine connection. The statue stood as a guardian to temple entrances and remained buried for centuries until its rediscovery.

Great Achievements

Ramses II, known as Ramses the Great, ruled for over 60 years and expanded Egypt’s empire deep into Nubia and the Levant. He built monumental structures such as Abu Simbel and the Ramesseum, leaving behind one of the most architecturally rich legacies in Egyptian history.

Discovery & Location

Discovered near the ancient city of Memphis in the 19th century, the statue was relocated to the Grand Egyptian Museum in Giza. Today, it stands proudly in the museum’s grand hall, welcoming visitors from all over the world.
