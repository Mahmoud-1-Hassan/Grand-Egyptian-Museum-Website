Statue of Khafre

Story & Age

Building the second pyramid at Giza (often called the Pyramid of Khafre) and possibly commissioning the Great Sphinx, which sits in front of his pyramid complex.

The statue

often called Khafre Enthroned — depicts Pharaoh Khafre seated on a throne; it dates to the Old Kingdom, 4th Dynasty (around ~2558–2532 BC)

Discovery & Location

The statue was unearthed in 1860 by Auguste Mariette, the first director of the Egyptian Antiquities Service. Its find-spot was the valley temple of Khafre’s pyramid complex on the Giza Plateau — the same complex connected to the causeway leading down to the Nile, and adjacent to the area of the Great Sphinx of Giza. Specifically, the statue was found “buried in a pit dug under the floor” of that valley temple. Archaeologists believe the statue was originally one among roughly 23 seated statues of Khafre placed in niches around the temple — but over time, almost all were destroyed or lost; this one survived intact
